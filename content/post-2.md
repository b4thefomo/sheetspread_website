<p><b>Step-by-Step Guide: Connecting Salesforce to Google Sheets in 5 Minutes</b></p>

<p>Here‚Äôs a detailed walkthrough of how to connect Salesforce to Google Sheets using SheetSpread:</p>

<ol>  <li><b>Install SheetSpread:</b>  <ul>  <li>Open Google Sheets and create a new spreadsheet.</li>  <li>Click on "Extensions" in the menu bar, then select "Add-ons" and "Get add-ons."</li>  <li>Search for "SheetSpread" in the <a href="https://workspace.google.com/marketplace" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Google Workspace Marketplace</a>.</li>  <li>Click on "Install" and follow the prompts to grant the necessary permissions. Ensure you understand and approve the permissions requested, as they are necessary for SheetSpread to access your Google Sheets and connect to Salesforce.</li>  </ul>  </li>  <li><b>Connect to Salesforce:</b>  <ul>  <li>Once SheetSpread is installed, click on "Extensions" in the menu bar again, and select "SheetSpread."</li>  <li>Click on "Launch" to open the SheetSpread sidebar.</li>  <li>In the sidebar, click on "Connect to Salesforce."</li>  <li>You will be prompted to log in to your <a href="https://login.salesforce.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Salesforce account</a>. Enter your Salesforce username and password.</li>  <li>Grant SheetSpread access to your Salesforce data.</li>  <li>Choose your Salesforce environment (Production or Sandbox). Using a Sandbox environment for testing is highly recommended.</li>  </ul>  </li>  <li><b>Select Your Data:</b>  <ul>  <li>Once connected, you can choose to retrieve data using either a predefined report or a custom SOQL query.</li>  <li><b>Using a Report:</b> Select "Use Salesforce Report" and choose the report you want to import from the dropdown menu. This is the easiest option for pre-configured Salesforce reports.</li>  <li><b>Using SOQL:</b> Select "Use SOQL Query" and enter your SOQL query in the text box. This option gives you more flexibility to retrieve specific data based on your requirements. For example: <br>  <code>SELECT Id, Name, Account. Name, CloseDate, StageName, Amount FROM Opportunity WHERE StageName = 'Closed Won' AND CloseDate = LAST_MONTH</code>  This query retrieves the ID, Name, Account Name, Close Date, Stage Name, and Amount for all Opportunities that were closed won in the last month.</li>  <li><b>Example SOQL Query (Leads):</b> <code>SELECT Id, FirstName, LastName, Company, Email, Status FROM Lead WHERE CreatedDate = TODAY</code> - This query retrieves leads created today.</li>  </ul>  </li>  <li><b>Configure Data Import:</b>  <ul>  <li>Specify the spreadsheet and sheet where you want to import the data.</li>  <li>Choose whether to include column headers in the import.</li>  <li>Configure the data refresh schedule. Select from options like "Manually," "Daily," "Weekly," or "Monthly." For automated reporting, schedule refreshes at appropriate intervals (e. g., daily for real-time dashboards, weekly for sales performance reports).</li>  <li>Choose whether to clear the sheet before importing new data. Selecting "Yes" will remove existing data before each refresh, ensuring you have the latest data. Selecting "No" will append new data to the existing data.</li>  </ul>  </li>  <li><b>Import Your Data:</b>  <ul>  <li>Click on the "Import Data" button.</li>  <li>SheetSpread will execute the query and import the data into your Google Sheet.</li>  <li>The data will appear in your specified sheet, ready for analysis and reporting.</li>  <li>Verify that the data is imported correctly and that the column headers are aligned as expected.</li>  </ul>  </li>
</ol>
